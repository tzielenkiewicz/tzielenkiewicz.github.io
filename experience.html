Priceless experience

Scientist

17 years with calorimeters, spetrometers, chromatographs, flasks, heaters, coolers. Dozens of scientific papers, reports, proposals. Time to explosion, steam gun, extractives content, wood porosity, XRF mapping, calibration. Many successes and losses. I studied explosives stability, heavy metals content in wood and biomass processing to biofuels. Big thing. But no more. This chapter of my life is closed now. The key is hidden. Deeply.

Lecturer

In parallel to scientific activity I was an academic teacher. And I was quite good. I liked students, I am not sure if mutually. I could start a lecture being not quite sure what to say and finish it after an hour realizing that I want to tell them much more, but there is no time left. When I was giving a lecture, I felt like an artist on the scene, nomen omen. After some time I rejected stress related to conference presentations (most of them were international) and liked that as well. I think I miss lecturing the most from "my previous life"...

Driver

When time for a change had come, I decided to try something completely different. I became a bus driver. I love that feeling of having such a big mashine under complete control. After three months of practice in some "normal" place (Warsaw) I "jumped into the deep water" and went far away to the north. I started to work in arctic Norway. Extremely responsible job, especially in specific weather conditions in that part of fjords country. Driving on the ice, in the fog, rain, snow and sun sharply shining during the same day was nothing extraordinary there. I met a few incredible guys. After a year I moved to London. Narrow streets and stressed people were my main opponents up there...

Singer

Yeeeeez, there was such a period in my life! Ten years on the scene, sometimes twice a week. Taverns, festivals, charity concerts and... rock and roll! We were playing shanties standards and later ourown songs drifting to other types of music (finally we called it 'punk-folk'). I was singing, playing the guitar and harmonica, writing songs... My God, I miss it!
